REDEEMED CHRISTIAN CHURCH OF GOD

The Redeemed Christian Church of God (RCCG) is a global Pentecostal Christian denomination that originated in Nigeria. It was founded in 1952 by Reverend Josiah Olufemi Akindayomi, who started with a small prayer group. The church grew steadily under his leadership. In 1981, Pastor Enoch Adejare Adeboye became the General Overseer of RCCG after Akindayomi's passing. Under Adeboye's leadership, RCCG experienced exponential growth both within Nigeria and internationally. The church adopted a more aggressive evangelistic approach and established a strong presence in various countries. RCCG places emphasis on prayer, holiness, and evangelism. It is known for its conventions, revivals, and programs that attract millions of people worldwide. The church's headquarters, Redemption Camp, located in Lagos, Nigeria, is one of the largest Christian auditoriums globally. The RCCG has played a significant role in promoting Christianity and social initiatives in Nigeria and beyond. It's known for its conservative theological stance and commitment to community development, education, and humanitarian efforts.
